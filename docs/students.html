Biotic interchange during a major continental collision: Sunda and Sahul

PhD Masters Honours Macroevolution Biogeography

Supervisor: Alex Skeels

The collision of the Sunda and Sahul tectonic plates created one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth. This project investigates how species have exchanged between these two landmasses over geological time, and what factors — including climate, ecology, and geography — have driven or impeded biotic interchange across Wallace’s Line. Students will use phylogenetic comparative methods and biogeographic modelling. See: Skeels et al. (2023) Science 381:86.

Deep-time climate effects on extinction patterns from molecular phylogenies

PhD Masters Honours Macroevolution Phylogenetics

Supervisor: Alex Skeels

How have past climate changes shaped the extinction and diversification of species over millions of years? This project uses time-calibrated molecular phylogenies to investigate the signatures of ancient climate-driven extinction events across plant and animal lineages.

Simulating the evolution of biodiversity in Australia

PhD Macroecology Biogeography Macroevolution

Supervisor: Alex Skeels

Australia’s extraordinary biodiversity is the product of millions of years of environmental change — most importantly, the progressive aridification of the continent and the consequent restructuring of biome distributions. This PhD project will use population- and species-level simulation models to understand how these changes have shaped the diversity, endemism, and phylogenetic structure of Australian plants and animals. Key modelling tools include Gen3sis (Hagen et al. 2021, PLOS Biology) and DReAD (Skeels & Cardillo 2019, American Naturalist) — both population- and species-level mechanistic models, not individual-based.
